  1. Description

    A Full Stack Developer is a versatile career specializing in many aspects of creating web content and applications. Full Stack Developers use many different languages such as Javascript, HTML, CSS, SSMS, PHP and more. They tend to develop both the User Interface and the supporting back-end logistics

  2. Required Skills

    The required skills vary depending on the company and on the project but in general, you will need a 2-year IT-related Diploma to a 4-year IT-related Bachelor's Degree. You will need to be proficient in javascript, css, html, ssms, php, github and more. Most companies have project-tracking software that you will need to be familiar with to be a valued member of a team.

  3. Salary

    The average salary for Full Stack Developers is $91,156 /year or approximately $39.38 /hour.

  4. Benefits/Insurance

    Benefits vary depending on the company you work for but you can expect the following benefits:
    • 3 weeks paid vacation
    • A health spending account anywhere from $250-$1500 a year
    • Dental coverage up to $1500 for required procedures and cleanings
    • Vision coverage for up to $750 every 2 years.
    • RRSP Matching
    • Stock Options
    • Paid Sick Days and Personal Days

  5. Work-Life Balance

    The work-life balance for a Full Stack Developer is considered well-balanced. You can expect to have 40-50 hour work weeks most Federal Statutory holidays off and generous paid time off. Of course, the IT industry is known for its tight dead-lines and overtime pushes but the compensation is generally worth it to most people.

  6. Average Job Satisfaction Levels

    According to Payscale.com, the average job satisfaction is rated at 4.01 out of 5 giving it a highly satisfied rating. Careerexplorer.com also shows that the average Full Stack Developer is satisfied with their career choice with the average of all polls being 3.5 out of 5, meaning that the large majority find their career choice very satisfying.

  7. Potential Employers

    Companies that employ Full Stack Developers:
    • Facebook/Meta
    • Netflix
    • AirBnB
    • Twitter
    • Lots of various smaller companies:
      • Anyone with a website can be potential clients/employers

  8. Job Location

    Based on the most recent State of Remote Engineering Survey/Report, it shows that 66% of global (non-US) engineers report that they are working fully remotely, where as last year during the come down of the covid pandemic 82% of engineers reported they were fully remote. In general, being a software related job it is quite flexible as to where you are working, this of course depends on what company you are working for. It is becoming more and more common for companies to adopt a sort of hybrid work schedule where you are required to come into the office a minimum amount of days in a week.

  9. Job Demand

    There is a growing demand for full stack software engineers the more the software market grows, the current market continues to grow as more applications and services are being built every day. This will require developers to maintain the applications that are being built to support a long term role for full stack engineers.

  10. Day-In-The-Life

    A full stack software developer can vary, but normally it starts with a quick brief or meeting in the morning to go over what a team is working on, what you need to complete the current task, what obstacles might be in the way of completing that task, etc. The tasks can be a range of things, this can include client meetings, writing or reviewing code, or researching / designing new applications and technologies.

  1. Description

    A UX/ UI Designer is responsible for designing or creating the graphical user interface (gui) on a website or application. They gather data about what the user requirements are and tailor the design to those needs, this means evaluating who and why they are using the application. They can design anything from logos and backgrounds to menu / widget layouts. The UX part of the job description is responsible for the user experience, and UI is responsible for the interface and look of the app.

  2. Required Skills

    • Previous experience as a ui/ux designer or a portfolio of similar projects.
    • Knowledge of common illustration or design tools like the adobe suite, figma InVision or any subsequent software
    • Usually a BFa / BSc in Design, Computer science related field
    • Having a team spirit and being able to collaborate with stakeholders.

  3. Salary

    The base pay range for a UI / UX Designer is from $54k - $79k. The average salary though is $69,000. This is for people just starting off, pay will tend to increase through experience.

  4. Benefits/Insurance

    It can depend on the company but most UI / UX Designers will have:
    • Health Insurance
    • Some kind of retirement plan
    • Paid time off
    • Performance-based bonuses
    • Profit sharing from a company

  5. Work-Life Balance

    It will primarily rely on the organization you work for. Although you will be collaborating with other designers and developers as a UI/UX Designer - which will leave you working longer than you expected - you will have set vacation days and flexible hours.

  6. Average Job Satisfaction Levels

    According to Careerkarma.com, designers rated their satisfaction 3.98 stars out of 5 stars which is a proficient satisfaction rate. While it’s a good rate it still shows that designers still have some complaints about their job.

  7. Potential Employers

    • IBM
    • Amazon
    • RBC
    • Google
    • Questrade
    • Autodesk

  8. Job Location

    There are options for hybrid positions but most designers just work from home.

  9. Job Demand

    According to the Occupations Alberta website, UI/UX or Web Designers are highly in demand in the province of Alberta. The demand for UI/UX Designers is expected to increase as corporations continue to grow and evolve in the current digital age.

  10. Day-In-The-Life

    A typical day for a UI/UX Designer consists of checking emails/messages, working on designs and other related elements, communicating with the client and working on creating the idea they have visualized, meeting with their team and boss, etc. UI/UX Designers are also not strangers to overtime and are known to constantly work past office hours to meet deadlines.

  1. Description

    An SEO (Search Engine Optimization) Strategist is someone who researches, collects and analyzes data from a website for the purpose of maximizing traffic volume to a website on any given search engine; SEO Strategists also aim to increase a website’s ranking on a search engine’s result page. They study the patterns and preferences of consumers and through the use of SEO tools and collaboration with other roles, such as Web Developers and Graphic Designers, make changes and updates to increase consumer traffic flow to a website.

  2. Required Skills

    SEO Strategists have a lot of different responsibilities so there isn’t a single specific skillset that they must possess but they should have traits/skills such as logical/creative thinking, attention to detail, multi-tasking, communication, quick problem solving, effective time management, and many more important skills. Additionally, although accepted education levels vary per company, SEO Strategists are expected to have a minimum 2 year Diploma or 4 year degree in the appropriate field(s) completed.

  3. Salary

    The average hourly wage for an SEO Strategist in Alberta is $25.64 and the average yearly salary is $54,016.

  4. Benefits/Insurance

    Most if not all corporations offer employee benefits and insurance. If you are a self-employed SEO Strategist, that might look different. However, SEO Strategists working for a company can expect to receive vision, dental, and medical insurance. Along with, paid time off, vacation hours/days, etc. Some companies offer stock options and RRSP matching as well.

  5. Work-Life Balance

    Work-life balance can vary extremely depending on the company you work for and the type of jobs they take. For example, a client who wants updates on everything on a day-to-day basis or every hour can put SEO strategists into working more hours than expected (overtime hours) and be heavily focused on their job rather than a balance with their personal life; however, another client can have a more relaxed approach to the job given and allow SEO Strategists to have good workflow and personal-life balance.

  6. Average Job Satisfaction Levels

    Based on data gathered by Payscale, people are highly satisfied with their SEO jobs rating it an average of 4/5.

  7. Potential Employers

    • Directive
    • BlueHat Marketing
    • Rush Ventures Online Marketing
    • Square One Insurance Services Inc.

  8. Job Location

    SEO Strategists have the option to work in an office, out of office, or even work from home. Required workplace may vary between companies. Most companies are based outside of Alberta, therefore there will be SEO Strategists that will be working in remote specialized office buildings typically and some will be self-employed and working from home or remote locations.

  9. Job Demand

    Demand for workers in this job is high, with about ~3600 already currently employed in Alberta and ~58 new positions suggested from data gathered by Alberta Regional Occupational Demand 2019-2023.

  10. Day-In-The-Life

    Typically, SEO Strategists will be building SEO strategies, researching competitive keywords, researching their competition (websites, weaknesses, strengths, etc), audit website technicalities, find and use optimizing tactics, research current trends, and work closely with clients and content creators to further performance.
